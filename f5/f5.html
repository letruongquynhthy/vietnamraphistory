Small beefs in 2016-2017

The period 2016 - 2017 had many intense beefs between large organizations, individual rappers and even within groups. Names like LK, B Ray, MC ILL, Torai9 all had outstanding beefs, while UG9, 7LD, Infamous continuously clashed with each other. The rap community and rap fans’ discussions on Facebook became extremely lively, creating endless debates.

LK vs. the southside

In early 2016, LK, along with LadyKillah Entertainment & GVR, released the track "Bắc" featuring LK, T-Akayz, BDT, Da Vickie and It's Lee. Immediately after, the southside counterattacked with the track "Bắc Tiến" by Southside Soldiers (3S), "Trung Trị" by Roy P, Bitu Midside, DK, and "Hưng Vong" by AE Cầu Giấy (Lil Wind, Master Of Word, Zed). However, LK did not respond, causing the beef to quickly die down.

LJ vs. Andree Right Hand, Binz, LK

After the success of the MV "Rice", LJ continued to release "Nằm Trên", which contained words criticizing Andree, Binz, LK. All three only reacted on Facebook without releasing a track. Binz posted a clip explaining his personal views, Andree showed off his income and criticized haters, and LK posted lyrics of a new music project with content criticizing LJ.

Not long after, LJ continued to release the track "3 Thằng 1 Dây", but LK and Binz remained silent. Andree personally criticized LJ in a demo performed in Đà Nẵng city, but did not release the full track. The beef temporarily died down, but only a few months later, it flared up again when Baby Red had a conflict with LK on Facebook. Immediately after that, Baby Red, LJ and D N together released "LK0BD", dissing LK and not forgetting to criticize Andree, Binz, Lil Shady, Sơn Tùng M-TP.

NPP (a.k.a Z) vs. S-Fury (FSR, 5Side)

The beef started when NPP aka Z diss LJ & S-Fury with "Nằm Lên". S-Fury responded with "Nữ Thành Nam", then NPP continued with "Gửi Thằng Em", but both did not continue to release tracks, causing the beef to gradually calm down.

Sevenk vs. DaBee, Hale, Amigo (Infamous)

Sevenk started the beef with "Lý Cây Roi", which was countered by DaBee and Hale with the track "Thất Kinh (Chị 7 & Anh 5)". Not willing to give up, Sevenk responded with "Reply Hộ", before Amigo continued with "La Thị Bảy", ending the beef.

Scott (SGM) vs. many underground rappers

Scott released the track "Underground", dissing many famous rappers. Immediately after, a series of counter-attack tracks appeared, including "Con Hư Tại Mẹ" by Bin QN, "Thằng Ngáo Keo Chó" by T.Boss (7LD), The M (Infamous) and Mr.Cười (UG9). In this track, Mr.Cười also criticized 5Side, causing 5Side to respond with the track "Movement" by Butcher and Z.4.Y.

UG9 & 7LD vs. the MV "G-Killah" (by LK, Lee7, BDT)

When the MV "G-Killah" was released in early July 2016, it received a large number of dislikes from the rap community, especially the northside. The two groups UG9 & 7LD simultaneously responded with the track "UG Killers" by Mose, Jo.T, Ez.A, TVT, Q.ABoys, Lil Andy and "Dizz G Killah" by M.A.X x T.Boss.

Rick (V-O) & S-Fury (FSR, 5Side) vs. MinhphucPK (HLBz)

Beef erupted when Rick & S-Fury dissed MinhphucPK for starting vlogs about rap online. MinhphucPK responded with the track "Chắc Đủ Tuổi", but Rick backed out, leaving it to S-Fury, who continued with "Vì Mày Tuổi Lồn". After that, MinhphucPK's homies released the track "Chỏ Nhẹ", dissing Rick & S-Fury.

The situation became more tense when MinhphucPK criticized S-Fury in his no.8 vlog, causing S-Fury to immediately react with a dissing clip on Facebook. This attracted many outside rappers to join in, such as LD with "Xàm Lông 6" and "Cái CC", NNT and Cường with "Nhỏ Nhẹ", and Kaoz and KuNz with "Chó Màu".

Phúc Du vs. Lăng LD (formerly as LangzLD) & Black Rin

Phúc Du started the beef with the track "Chắc Chưa", which was countered by Lăng LD & Black Rin with "W.T.C". Black Rin continued to diss Phúc Du with "Câm Mồm Phúc Du", making the beef more and more tense.

Torai9 vs. many underground rapper

In July 2017, Torai9 (FSR) made a comeback with the track "Dẹp Mẹ Đi", aimed at many famous rappers and trends in the underground scene, causing a stir in the community. After that, he also met some people mentioned in the track such as Huỳnh James, Pjnboys, Đen.

Queen B vs. Foxie

A conflict broke out on Facebook between Queen B and Foxie, called the the "Cáo Con vs B Bitch" war. Queen B released "Cáo Phó" to diss Foxie, and not long after, Foxie responded with "Queen B Dissing". The two continued to attack each other on Facebook but did not release any new tracks.

B Ray vs. Rhymastic (2017)

Beef between B Ray (with Young H, Acy, LJ,...) and Rhymastic (with ICD, Sushi, Ravenmen,...)

This beef stemmed from a conflict between Young H and Black Murder, and involved rappers from both northside and southside.

Phase 1: Initiating the beef

Before that, B Ray and Young H returned to Vietnam and had an argument with Black Murder on Facebook. Afterwards, Black Murder went to the 95G headquarters to meet Young H, leading to a physical fight that caused damage to both sides, even 95G was affected (the members were not involved). The incident was later resolved.

Immediately after that, Rhymastic posted a status criticizing Young H and B Ray, saying that they hated the government authorities and the police, but when they encountered problems, they asked the police to solve it for them. This status caused a strong reaction from the community, escalating the conflict. Rhymastic responded with the track "Bản Kiểm Điểm", officially igniting the beef.

Phase 2: Rappers entering the beef

Right after "Bản Kiểm Điểm", VickyBraak released "When I'm Coming", while Acy counterattacked with "Tiền Lệ Nguy Hiểm". Diikayz also joined in with "Lập Biên Bản", continuing to target Rhymastic. At the same time, Mr. Cười released the track "LJBD", dissing LJ.

Shortly after, Rhymastic responded with "Ngược Đời", but Acy refused to give up, continuing to counterattack with "Thể Loại Nhàn Hạ". LJ also joined the battle with the track “Một Miếng".

Phase 3: Northside strikes back strongly

Protesting against regional discrimination, young rapper Shushi released "From Northside", dissing VickyBraak. At the same time, Sputnik, another young rapper, also released "Hòn Ngọc Biển Đông".

ICD returned with "Back To Da Game 2", dissing Young H, B Ray, VickyBraak and LJ. Meanwhile, B Ray officially dissed Rhymastic with "U Don't Know Shit", and Rhymastic quickly responded with "Bray Dissin".

Phase 4: Climax and the final disses

In response to criticism that Acy's rap lines were too long, Acy responded with "Trả Lời Người Hỏi". Ravenmen and cRazzilit from the northside collaborated and released "An Ninh Thủ Đô", while LJ and S-Fury dissed Rhymastic and ICD together with "Khỏi Cản".

The beef continued with the confrontation between B Ray and Rhymastic, when B Ray released the track "Bố Mày Từ Thiện" (ft. Young H), and Rhymastic counterattacked with "Ếch Báo Dissin". ICD continued to attack B Ray with "Back To Da Game 5".

Finally, B Ray ended the beef with the track "Hành Thiện", bringing the war to a close.

B Ray vs. The Night (2017)

Beef between B Ray and The Night

This beef broke out because B Ray had previously criticized The Night in many other beefs, typically when confronting Rick and Ricky Star. In addition, The Night also wanted to cause some attention after a period of stop rapping.

Phase 1: Starting the beef

The Night was the one who initiated it first, provoking B Ray with the track "32 Bars". Immediately, B Ray responded with "Mắt Tím Môi Đỏ 2", officially opening the beef.

Phase 2: Tensed battle

After B Ray's first response, The Night continued with "Chạy Đâu Con Sâu", but not long after, B Ray counterattacked strongly with the track "Bảo Kiếm Không Ra". Just one day later, The Night continued to reply with "Kiếm Đâu Có Ra", maintaining the tense situation.

Phase 3: End of beef

Not letting the battle drag on, B Ray released the track "Hổng Nhây Nữa Nha", ending the beef with The Night.

Phase 4: Homies joining the fight

After B Ray and The Night ended, The Night's homies from 64 Bars continued the fight. Pompey released the track "Chạy Đâu Con Sâu 2" to diss B Ray.

Immediately, B Ray's V-O side counterattacked with "Có Con Gà Trong Sân" by S.O.S. In addition, Rush from V-O released the track "BOM BAY", while Sir B released "Bắt Chó", targeting Pompey, pushing the beef to a climax.

Highlights

in the underground and mainstream scene (2017)

Underground's success explosion

Following the success of 2016, 2017 marked the peak of underground, with a series of hit tracks that were widely known to the audience. "Quăng Tao Cái Boong" by Huỳnh James & Pjnboys became a phenomenon, reaching nearly 59 million views on YouTube (as of June 28, 2017). The track "Liên Hoàn Dizz" by Bé Cường got popular with unique lyrics, especially the phrase "trời đất dung hoa, vạn vật sinh sôi", although this catch phrase only existed for a short time. In addition, Sol x Yuno x Kaka$hi released the track "Con Tim Tao Đau Quá Man", which quickly became an online phenomenon. Another hit was "Buồn Của Anh" by Đạt G x Masew x K-ICM, which continuously ranked at the top of the Zing chart.

Đen Vâu and his contribution to underground

Among the artists who contributed to bringing rap closer to the public, Đen Vâu stands out the most when he continuously released quality music products with content close to the audience. He released "Đi Theo Bóng Mặt Trời" (ft. Giang Nguyễn) and "Ta Cứ Đi Cùng Nhau" (ft.Linh Cáo), receiving great support from fans.

The success of producers

Not only rappers, 2017 was also a great year for mixers, especially Masew. He produced many big hits such as "Đường Một Chiều" (Magazine x Huỳnh Tú), "Ở Trong Thành Phố" (B Ray x Hannah B) and "Túy Âm" (Xesi x Nhatnguyen).

Conflict between G5R and OTD

At the end of 2017, a conflict broke out between two southside rap organizations, G5R and OTD. According to Jombie (G5R), the cause was due to the YouTube accounts of Jombie, 95G, OTD, Ếch and Báo being hacked. The incident escalated when G5R and OTD arranged to meet in Cần Thơ city to resolve the issue but could not meet. Afterwards, Jombie (G5R), Ricky Star and Lăng LD (OTD) posted a counter-attack clip, blaming each other for the cause of the conflict.

Rap organizations' personnel changes

April and May had major personnel changes in a number of rap organizations. OTD officially parted ways with Pjpo (he was the co-founder of OTD), while GVR announced that Táo, D.I.E Team, Deezy and Olia Hoàng had joined the organization as artists.

Freestyle battle contest "Thằng Cha Mày"

As mentioned in the F3 section, following the success of the previous season, Clog Hiếu & Huy continued to organize the freestyle battle tournament "Thằng Cha Mày" in July at VHS Cafe. Pjpo and The Night were invited to be the main judges, attracting a large number of rap fans to participate. However, during the tournament, some rap fanpages livestreamed and posted clips of the matches on Facebook without permission, causing the organizers to react harshly. After that, the fanpages were forced to remove the videos upon request. The final results of the tournament were also controversial, forcing Sol'Bass to speak up to explain.

Show "Ếch & Báo: Reunion" and the disbandment of EvB Records

After being established, EvB Records organized the show "Ếch & Báo: Reunion" in Sóc Trăng (with G5R) and in Hồ Chí Minh city. However, after only a short time of operation, EvB announced its disbandment, so that the members could focus on their personal projects.

Government control on the rap underground community

The end of 2017 was a difficult period for underground, when some rap shows and tournaments were controlled by the government. B Ray's show "Điện Tử Thẻ và Truyện Tranh" was banned by the Department of Culture and Performance. Similarly, the freestyle battle "Bạn Có Tài Mà!" was also banned. Some rappers and fans believed that B Ray was the cause, because he had previously released many politically sensitive tracks, which affected the rap community. This led to fierce debates between fans and rappers in the industry.

The birth of underground talk shows

2017 marked the appearance of two professional rap talk shows, including "Underground Talk" by Nguyễn Gia Minh (Sói UGuyz) and "V.Sik Talks" by Trần Gia Hào (Callous). These two channels officially launched with the hottest interviews of the time with Young H and B Ray, when they were hosting the show "Ếch & Báo: Reunion".

Mainstream continues to grow strongly

Not only underground, the mainstream rappers also has many notable marks. Rhymastic released "Yêu 5", reaching more than 43 million listens after 5 months, reaching the Top 3 of Zing Chart for many weeks and being in the Top 5 best songs of the year at Zing Music Awards.

In addition, RichChoi released the track "Real Rap", receiving great support from the rap fan community.

B Ray vs. Richchoi

(2017)

Beef between B Ray and Richchoi

Phase 1: Initiating the beef

The beef began when RichChoi became famous thanks to "Real Rap" and decided to diss B Ray with the track "Babe Get My Gun", both to strengthen his name and reputation and also because B Ray had made a joke about him. Not letting it go, B Ray immediately counterattacked with "Little Homie", officially erupting the beef.

Phase 2: Tensed battle

After "Little Homie", RichChoi continued to release the track "3 Điều Đức Dạy Bảo" in response. B Ray quickly responded with the track "Thất Đức", keeping the fight fair and balanced.

Phase 3: End and reconciliation

Shortly after, RichChoi released the track "Đạo Đức", sharing his thoughts on the beef. After this track, the two decided to make peace, posting a message to stop the beef on Facebook.

However, despite declaring reconciliation, RichChoi continued to diss B Ray in a verse of the track "3 Con Chó Ngọng 2". This made the conflict between the two sides to not fully end.

Phase 4: Face-to-face talk

After a long period of tension, B Ray and RichChoi met at a cafe to talk face-to-face and officially reconcile.

Aftermath of the beef

Although the beef ended, one of the most memorable parts was the line “Má mày tên là Ngọc, ông già mày tên là Tuấn Anh", translated to "Your mother's name is Ngọc, your father's name is Tuấn Anh" from B Ray's track "Thất Đức" became a trend. The names "Ngọc" and "Tuấn Anh" were constantly teased by the audience and even used as a fact to diss RichChoi in many beefs later.

Northside vs. Southside

(2018)

Beef between the northside and southside

The beef between northside and southside in 2018 was one of the biggest confrontations in Vietnamese rap history, attracting many famous organizations and rappers, and producing two record-breaking tracks for the longest and shortest tracks in Vietnamese rap.

Phase 1: Escalating conflict

The conflict between OTĐ and RVP became tense when Seachains and Vinadu had a disagreement, while Vinadu had previously beefed with Lăng LD and Black Rin. Phúc Du posted a status claiming that Seachains had a regional discrimination attitude, while MC ILL wrote an controversial status, sparking a wave of discussions.

Things became more tense when Pjpo released the track "Bằng Track", a reply for Rhymastic's "Phán Xét". Phúc Du thought that Pjpo’s track was aimed at RVP, so that same night, he released "Cần Cẩu" to diss Pjpo and those who were claimed of discrimanting regions, making the north-south beef tenser than ever.

Phase 2: The two regions officially confront each other

After the opening fire, many rappers from both regions joined the battle with a series of solo tracks:

Southside counterattacked with "From The South" (M.A.X), "Chiến Thư" (Obito) and "Không Có Diss Ai Hết Nha Tui Theo Chủ Trương Hòa Bình Chết Mẹ Lộn Cái Này Là Title" (Sol'Bass)

Northside responded with "Tiếu Ngạo Giang Hồ" (Hades, dissing Ếch & Báo, Pjpo), "3 Con Chó Ngọng 2" (RichChoi, continued to diss southside)

Hades released the track "Rep Bass" to respond to Sol'Bass, while Lil Lighthouse released "Vạch Trần" to reply Hades

Sol'Bass did not delay, releasing the track "Lag/Lác" to respond to RichChoi, while Frakez fired at RVP with "Nã Đạn"

RichChoi continued to fight back with "Bát Cứt", and HH from RVP released "Chiến Email" to diss Obito. MC43 from northside also released the track "Dẹp Loạn Sứ Quân" to continue attacking Obito

Phase 3: The war spreads

In addition to the main rappers, many lesser-known rappers also joined in:

Bica released "Round 2", dissing MC ILL & Phúc Du

Arius released "Hạt Tiêu Bắc", aimed directly at Pjpo

Rick (V-O) joined the battle with "Listen Bucky!", but declared that he only wanted to compare his skills, not to directly diss someone

Tony TK and Torai9 simultaneously entered the battle with "Fuck Vinagang" and "2 Thằng Phế Vật"

RichChoi continued to target Rick, Torai9 and Tony TK with the track "3 Con Chó Ngọng 3". Southside responded strongly with "Việc", gathering many rappers from the midside, eastside, westside and southside such as Roki x TripK x Nhạc Bụi x Obito x MR.J x Finnizzle x Jio x Kain x SirB

At this time, Pjpo officially joined the battle with the track "Sao Bắt Kỳ Thế", dissing RichChoi, MC ILL, Phúc Du and Rhymastic

Phase 4: The climax of the war

After Pjpo’s release, MC ILL - RVP's leader, released "Hồng Lâu Mộng" to respond, receiving praise from Sol'Bass

On the same day, Torai9 released "Nép Qua Một Bên", dissing RichChoi but still had good intentions to praise northside. Not long after, Phúc Du released "Rượu Phạt", targeting Pjpo and Sol'Bass. BlackBi also joined in with the track "Giải Trí Chút", dissing Phúc Du and Hưng Cao (MC ILL’s real name.

From RVP, RichChoi released the track "Mỹ Hầu Vương", targeting Torai9, but then Torai9 announced to withdraw from the beef. However, many fans thought that RichChoi "weakened" after this track.

Not stopping there, Phúc Du continued with "Dựng Lên", a short 1 verse track to diss BlackBi. Rism & Trí Dũng (1337) and Yung Quất (S.K.U.L.L) released a series of tracks "Quy Luật Địa Lý" and "Quyết Đấu", targeting southside. BlackT also joined in with the track "Chút Muối", attacking Vinagang.

Some other rappers like Sinike also joined in, dissing RichChoi & Vuông SB with the track "Game Over"

Phase 5: Two record-breaking rap tracks were born & the beef ended

On the occasion of April 30 (Reunification Day), Acy released the track "Trường Ca 5B", 19:05 minutes long, setting a record for the longest rap track in Vietnam.

Not long after, Phúc Du broke this record with the track "Tứ Mã", 20 minutes long, responding to Acy. Acy continued to fight back with "Khúc Cầu Hồn", but by this time the beef had begun to cool down.

After that, Jombie and 2Can released the MV "Bắc Kì Trong Nam", expressing their views on reconciling the north and the south. Although the beef had cooled down, many rappers still occasionally attacked each other on Facebook.

Another record was also set: B Ray released the track "Tao Muốn Share Nhạc", dissing 125 northside rappers with only 1 line, becoming the shortest rap track in Vietnam

Blacka

vs. B Ray (2018)

Beef between Blacka (a.k.a Black Murder) and B Ray

The beef between Blacka and B Ray was one of the notable confrontations in 2018, stemming from B Ray's subtle mention of Blacka and several other rappers in the track "Con Trai Cưng".

Phase 1: B Ray provokes the beef

B Ray collaborates with Masew and K-ICM to release "Con Trai Cưng", a satirical track with lyrics that poke fun at RichChoi, Blacka, and several other rappers. The track quickly becomes a phenomenon, but also causes B Ray to receive a lot of criticism from the rappers mentioned.

Phase 2: Blacka's strong counterattack

As soon as "Con Trai Cưng" was released, Blacka was prepared and quickly responded with the track "Bố Mày Biết Mà", showing a strong flow and strong lyrics, aimed straight at B Ray.

Phase 3: B Ray refuses to give up

After a period of silence, B Ray counterattacked with the track "Ân Xá", continuing to attack Blacka. Not long after, Blacka continued to respond strongly with the track "Chỉ Là Công Việc", emphasizing that this beef was just a work matter, not a personal one.

Phase 4: End of the beef

Not letting things stop, B Ray continued to release the track "Ân Cần", but at this time the interest of rap fans on the beef had begun to decrease. After a week, B Ray released the track "Calm Down", while Black Murder released the track "Đây Là Rap Việt", emphasizing his point of view on the rap game.

After that, the beef gradually cooled down and there were no more diss tracks from either side.

The rise of television rap shows

(2020)

Rap Việt

On August 1, Rap Việt, the first rap show organized on television in Vietnam is aired nationwide

Touliver was the music producer, two judges JustaTee and Rhymastic, and four coaches including Binz, Karik, Suboi, Wowy, and the host Trấn Thành.

King of Rap

Coincidentally, King Of Rap and Rap Việt both aired their first episode on the same day and ended their shows on the same day. These two shows were competing on different TV broadcast channels.

The mentor team includes Datmaniac, BigDaddy, LK and Lil’ Shady

On November 14, Rap Việt and King of Rap ended, with Dế Choắt crowned Rap Việt champion and ICD winning King of Rap. These two shows brought Vietnamese rap to the mainstream, attracting a huge audience.

However, after the first season, King Of Rap received less support from the general public than Rap Việt. Unfortunately, as of 2025, Rap Việt is the only rap show that continued to its forth season while King Of Rap only had one season since 2020.
